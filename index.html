Introducing AquaStream360

AquaStream360 is a comprehensive engineering software platform developed to help utilities effectively manage their water distribution systems and mitigate the impacts of numerous challenges affecting water infrastructure—including climate variability, population growth, aging assets, non-revenue water losses, and operational inefficiencies. It offers a powerful and integrated set of features that simplifies network model generation, analysis and empowers users to model, design, and evaluate a wide range of performance metrics with exceptional accuracy and speed, enabling them to develop effective strategies that ultimately enhance the resilience and long-term sustainability of their water distribution systems.

Designed For:

  • M – Modeling
  • O – Optimization
  • O – Operational Monitoring
  • N – Network Testing
  • S – Scenario-Based Simulations
  • U – Uncertainty Analysis
  • C – Criticality Analysis
  • K – Knowledge-Driven Decision Making
  • E – Enhancing Efficiency
  • R – Resilience Analysis and Risk Assessment

Key Features

  • Resilience Analysis

  • Network Performance Monitoring

About the Developer

AquaStream360 was created in 2025 by Prince K. Munsaka, a Civil and Water Engineering student at the National University of Science and Technology. The idea was born out of real-world challenges he observed during his industrial attachment in Mutare, Zimbabwe, where inefficient water distribution systems and data gaps hindered effective service delivery.

Motivated by a desire to make a tangible impact, Prince envisioned a solution that would blend engineering principles with modern technology. The result was AquaStream360—a platform designed to support water engineers and decision-makers through smarter, data-informed, automated solution.
